Hormonal Imbalance & More

Polycystic Ovary Syndrome (PCOS) is a hormonal and metabolic disorder that affects up to 13% of women of reproductive age. It is diagnosed based on a combination of irregular periods, elevated androgen levels, and polycystic ovaries on ultrasound.

Managing PCOS Effectively

Lifestyle Changes: Even a 5% weight reduction can improve symptoms.
Medications: Oral contraceptives, metformin, anti-androgens, and fertility treatments like letrozole or clomiphene.

üß¨ Q1: Is PCOS the same as infertility?

A: No, PCOS can cause difficulty in conceiving, but with the right treatment and lifestyle changes, many women with PCOS can become pregnant.

üç© Q2: Can I manage PCOS without medication?

A: Yes, lifestyle changes like a balanced diet, regular exercise, and stress management can help manage symptoms effectively.

üíä Q3: Are birth control pills a cure for PCOS?
